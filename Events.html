A01.EARTHDANCE NAIROBI 2017
DATE: Saturday, 29th September to Sunday, 20th September.
VENUE:Ngong Racecourse, Nairobi.
TIME:5:00PM to 6:00PM.

Earthdance Nairobi is back for 2017! The Kenyan edition of this global peace festival will be happening on Saturday 29th September to Sunday 20th at The Ngong Racecourse. This year features four stages, a first for the Kenyan electronic music festival scene. Along with the great music we will uphold the BYOB (Bring Your Own Bottle) bar system which was a big hit with Earthdancers last year. Prepare yourself for the best festival experience this year.

In 2017 Earthdance global celebrates 21 years of mind blowing events. Since 1997 this international event has featured in over 300 cities in more than 65 countries across the planet. We will join all other Earthdance events this year in celebration. Will you be celebrating too?

This year’s theme is the embodiment of today’s culture. It’s time for a universal Re-Evolution, moving us together through music and dance, towards our highest purpose of peace building and unity.

The stages at this year’s event pay a testament to the four worldly elements; Earth, Wind, Water and Fire. The Earth stage will host 6:AM Entertainment’s top talent in Electronic Dance music. The Fyah stage will have the Epic Nation crew's finest Urban and Hip Hop acts. The Water stage is dedicated to the Underground Electronic sound and last but not least the wind stage will feature Alternative and Tripped-out DJs.

A03. STORYMOJA FESTIVAL 2017
DATE:Sunday, 1st October.
VENUE:Nairobi National Museum, Museum Hill, Westlands.
TIME:10:00AM to 3:30PM.

Storymoja Festival prides itself as ‘the biggest book party’ in East Africa, and as a ‘celebration of books, stories and culture’. This year we look to reproduce the feeling of a grand book party, and a celebration of our stories and culture and literature, on a grander, even more, ambitious scale. We hope to become the biggest, most stimulating gathering of local & international writers, artists and audiences exploring, showcasing, generating and celebrating the African narrative in many creative and cultural forms.
​ ​
The 2017 program will have a full offering of book discussions, public lectures, master classes & workshops, art & theatre shows, concerts, teen talent, competitions, jobs & careers, schools, children’s, and family programs throughout a five-day program.

The Theme of this year's Storymoja Festival is Black Peace, in commemoration of the ten years that have passed since one of Kenya's dark moments - the 2007/08 Post Election Violence. In celebration of Black Peace, we are acknowledging ethnic, political and other forms of conflict that exist between us. It will therefore be interesting to see how this theme will play out in conversations on writing, art, theatre, film, fashion, policy, business & entrepreneurship, tourism, human rights, leadership, environment, disability, technology, women and youth empowerment, health, gender, identity and so on throughout the festival.
